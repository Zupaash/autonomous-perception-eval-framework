## Research Focus

Autonomous systems operate under strict temporal constraints, where delayed perception updates may have significant downstream effects on navigation and decision-making.

This project investigates the relationship between perception latency, latency variability, and deployment feasibility in real-time autonomous systems.

Rather than focusing solely on average throughput, the framework aims to characterize latency distributions and identify conditions under which perception pipelines become unsuitable for time-sensitive applications.

## Secondary Research Question

At what point do increases in perception quality fail to justify additional computational cost within real-time autonomous systems?
